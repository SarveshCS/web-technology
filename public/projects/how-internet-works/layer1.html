Layer 1: Physical Layer

The hardware foundation

What is the Physical Layer?

The Physical Layer is the lowest layer of the OSI model. It deals with the actual physical connection between devices - the cables, signals, and hardware that transmit raw bits (0s and 1s).

This layer is all about electricity, light, and radio waves - the actual means of moving data.

Key Functions

  • Bit Transmission: Converts data to electrical/optical/radio signals
  • Physical Topology: Defines network layout (bus, star, ring, mesh)
  • Data Rate: Determines transmission speed (bits per second)
  • Synchronization: Coordinates timing between sender and receiver
  • Physical Interface: Defines connectors, pins, voltages

Transmission Media

  • Twisted Pair Cable (Ethernet):
    • Cat5e, Cat6, Cat6a, Cat7
    • RJ-45 connectors
    • Up to 10 Gbps
  • Fiber Optic:
    • Uses light pulses
    • Single-mode and Multi-mode
    • Up to 100+ Gbps
  • Wireless:
    • Wi-Fi (2.4 GHz, 5 GHz, 6 GHz)
    • Bluetooth, Infrared
    • Radio waves

Common Devices

  • Hubs - Broadcasts to all ports (obsolete)
  • Repeaters - Amplifies signals
  • Network Interface Cards (NICs) - Connect devices to network
  • Cables & Connectors - Physical transmission medium
  • Modems - Converts digital to analog signals

Signal Types

Digital Signal: Discrete values (0 or 1)

Real-World Example

When you plug in an Ethernet cable:

1. Electrical signals travel through copper wires

2. Voltage changes represent 0s and 1s

3. NIC converts signals to digital data

4. Data passed up to Layer 2 for processing
